**Pro tip**: Use Find & Replace (Ctrl+H) in the editor!

### B. What Happened (2 minutes)

Write 2-3 paragraphs:

- **Paragraph 1**: How the breach occurred
- **Paragraph 2**: What the investigation found
- **Timeline**: List 3-5 key dates

**Example:**

```
On July 28, 2023, Wojeski & Company discovered a ransomware attack
when employees couldn't access files. Investigation revealed it was
caused by a phishing email.
```

### C. Data Exposed (30 seconds)

- Financial account numbers
- [Add others from source]

### D. Violations & Failures (2 minutes)

Split into two sections:

**Technical Failures:**

- No encryption
- Weak access controls
- Phishing vulnerability

**Compliance Failures:**

- Delayed notification
- Missing security policies

## üí° Pro Tips

### Finding Good Stories

**High Value Topics:**

- Large penalties ($50K+)
- Multiple violations
- Well-known companies
- Recent cases (last 30 days)
- Multiple states affected

### Speed Tricks

1. **Find & Replace All**: Use Ctrl+H to replace placeholders in bulk
2. **Copy Timeline**: Copy dates directly from press release
3. **Use Bullet Points**: Don't over-write, be concise
4. **Link Early**: Add source URL first so you don't forget

### Making It Unique

Don't just summarize! Add:

- **Industry context**: "This is the 3rd accounting firm this year..."
- **Expert opinion**: "As a compliance platform, we see..."
- **Actionable advice**: "Here's exactly what to do..."
- **State comparison**: "Unlike California, New York requires..."

## üÜò Troubleshooting

### "I can't find the breach details"

‚Üí Look for the full press release PDF, not just the summary

### "The timeline is confusing"

‚Üí Focus on 3 key dates: Breach, Discovery, Notification

### "There's no penalty amount"

‚Üí Look for "settlement", "agreed order", or "consent decree"

### "Multiple breaches mentioned"

‚Üí Choose the most recent or largest one

### "Template is overwhelming"

‚Üí Delete optional sections you don't need
